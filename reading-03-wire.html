Lesson 3：小大脑会连接到它的世界 Lesson 3: Little Brains Wire Themselves to Their World

儿童大脑通过真实经验塑造连接：环境、关系、语言，都会成为‘接线材料’。 Children’s brains wire to their world through experience—environment, relationships, and language are the ‘wiring material.’

概念卡Concept cards 讨论Discussion 练习Practice

Big Idea 核心概念Big Idea

成长并不是‘装进知识’，而是连接方式被经验反复塑形。稳定关系与可预测环境是关键。 Development isn’t ‘adding knowledge’—it’s repeated shaping of connection patterns. Stable relationships and predictable environments matter.

Concept Cards 概念卡Concept Cards

  • 经验（Experience）：输入的质量与频率会塑形连接。Experience (经验): Quality and frequency of inputs shape wiring.
  • 依恋/关系安全（Attachment）：安全关系降低防御，提高探索。Attachment (依恋/关系安全): Safety reduces defense and increases exploration.
  • 语言（Language）：语言为体验贴标签并组织记忆。Language (语言): Language labels experience and organizes memory.
  • 环境（Environment）：噪音/压力/混乱会消耗身体预算。Environment (环境): Noise/stress/chaos drains body budget.

理解展开Explanation

这也解释了：同一个孩子换学校/换国家/换文化时，需要的首先不是更难的题，而是重新建立‘可预测的日常’。 This explains why transitions (new school/country/culture) require predictable routines before harder academics.

讨论问题Discussion

  • 你见过孩子在‘更安全的关系’里突然变得更会学吗？Have you seen a child learn better in a safer relationship?
  • 你认为‘练习礼仪/祝福话’在塑造什么能力？What abilities are shaped by practicing etiquette/blessings?
  • 如果环境会接线，我们如何设计家庭/课堂的“可预测”？If environments wire brains, how do we design predictability at home/school?

生活练习Practice

  • 设计一个“3分钟稳定仪式”：开始学习前固定动作（喝水/整理桌面/写今日目标）。Create a 3-minute stabilizing ritual: water/desk reset/today’s goal.
  • 一周一次家庭复盘：哪一天最顺？为什么？下周怎么复制？Weekly family review: which day felt best? why? how to replicate?
  • 给孩子一个选择权：任务顺序/工具/同伴，降低防御。Offer choice (order/tools/partner) to lower defense.

教学应用（SEL × 学术）Teaching Application (SEL × Academics)

  • 把过渡期目标写成：安全感先于效率。Write transition goals as: safety before efficiency.
  • 用‘可预测’代替‘控制’：时间块、流程卡、结束语。Use predictability, not control: time blocks, process cards, closing lines.
  • 教学生英文表达：“I learn better when the routine is predictable.”Teach an English line: “I learn better when the routine is predictable.”
