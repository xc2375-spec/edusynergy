Lesson 1：你只有一个大脑（不是三个） Lesson 1: You Have One Brain (Not Three)

“理性脑 vs 情绪脑”不是三套系统的战争，而是一套大脑在不同情境下的整合工作。 It’s not a war between ‘rational’ and ‘emotional’ brains—one brain integrates in context.

概念卡Concept cards 讨论Discussion 练习Practice

Big Idea 核心概念Big Idea

情绪与认知并非独立模块。所谓“理性/情绪”是同一系统在不同任务与身体预算下的表现。 Emotion and cognition aren’t separate modules. ‘Rational/emotional’ are context-dependent modes of one system.

Concept Cards 概念卡Concept Cards

  • 整合（Integration）：大脑各区域协同完成任务。Integration (整合): Brain regions coordinate to accomplish tasks.
  • 建构（Construction）：大脑用过去经验建构当下体验。Construction (建构): The brain constructs experience from past experience.
  • 情感底色（Affect）：能量与唤醒水平影响注意与解释。Affect (情感底色): Energy/arousal colors attention and interpretation.
  • 情境（Context）：同一刺激在不同情境可被不同理解。Context (情境): Same stimulus can be interpreted differently by context.

理解展开Explanation

当我们说“别情绪化”，其实忽略了：情绪是信息与能量管理的一部分。更有效的是帮助学生识别状态并重建任务。 When we say “don’t be emotional,” we ignore that emotion is part of information and energy management. Better: help students name state and restructure tasks.

讨论问题Discussion

  • 你见过“越焦虑越学不进去”的例子吗？发生了什么？Have you seen ‘more anxiety, less learning’? What happened?
  • 当你很兴奋时，你更容易冲动还是更有创造力？When excited, do you become impulsive or creative—or both?
  • 你能否用“一个大脑”的视角重写一句‘别想太多’？Rewrite “don’t overthink” from a ‘one brain’ perspective.

生活练习Practice

  • 把一次冲突写成“状态—需要—策略”：我当时的状态是什么？我需要什么？我可以做什么？Rewrite one conflict as “state–need–strategy.” What state? What need? What next step?
  • 练习一句‘整合式自我对话’：我现在紧张，但我可以先做最小一步。Practice an integration self-talk line: “I’m tense, and I can start with the smallest step.”
  • 和家人做角色扮演：同一句话在不同语气/场合下的效果。Role-play: same words in different tone/context and observe effects.
小提示：先“只看中文”完成，再切“中英对照”对照表达，最后“只看英文”复述。 Tip: Try ZH-only → bilingual → EN-only for retelling and fluency.

教学应用（SEL × 学术）Teaching Application (SEL × Academics)

  • 用“状态语言”替代贴标签：把‘不认真’换成‘现在注意资源不足’。Replace labels with state language: ‘not trying’ → ‘attention resources are low.’
  • 设计两种作业入口：低预算（更短/更具体）与高预算（更开放/更挑战）。Offer two entry levels: low-budget (short/specific) vs high-budget (open/challenging).
  • 教学生英文表达：“My brain is trying to protect my energy; I need a clearer next step.”Teach an English line: “My brain is protecting my energy; I need a clearer next step.”
