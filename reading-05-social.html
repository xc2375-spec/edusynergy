Lesson 5：你的大脑在悄悄与他人大脑合作 Lesson 5: Your Brain Secretly Works with Other Brains

人类大脑天生是社会性的：我们在互动中同步、调节、共同建构意义。 Human brains are social by default—we sync, regulate, and co-construct meaning through interaction.

概念卡Concept cards 讨论Discussion 练习Practice

Big Idea 核心概念Big Idea

很多‘状态’并不是个人问题，而是关系系统的产物：被看见、被理解，会改变身体预算。 Many ‘states’ are relational: being seen and understood changes the body budget.

Concept Cards 概念卡Concept Cards

  • 共同调节（Co-regulation）：通过他人的稳定支持恢复状态。Co-regulation (共同调节): Regulating with support from others.
  • 社会预测（Social prediction）：预测他人意图与反应。Social prediction (社会预测): Predicting others’ intentions and responses.
  • 归属（Belonging）：归属感减少威胁感。Belonging (归属): Belonging reduces threat.
  • 信任（Trust）：信任降低防御、提升学习投入。Trust (信任): Trust lowers defense and increases engagement.

理解展开Explanation

当学生‘不配合’，可能是在保护自己免受羞耻或威胁。关系安全是学习前提，而不是额外加分项。 When students ‘resist,’ they may be protecting themselves from shame/threat. Relational safety is a prerequisite, not a bonus.

讨论问题Discussion

  • 你在哪位老师/同伴身边更敢提问？为什么？With which teacher/peer do you ask more questions—and why?
  • 哪些课堂语言会让学生身体预算下降？哪些会提升？What classroom language drains vs boosts body budget?
  • 如何把‘纠错’做成共同调节而不是羞辱？How can correction become co-regulation instead of shame?

生活练习Practice

  • 每次学习前找一个“共调节动作”：一起整理/一起画图/一起复述 60 秒。Before studying, do one co-regulation action: organize/diagram/60-sec recap together.
  • 练一句“请求支持”的英文：I’m stuck—can you help me find the next step?Practice an English ask: “I’m stuck—can you help me find the next step?”
  • 每周一次感谢：写下一个“被支持的瞬间”并复盘原因。Weekly gratitude: record one ‘supported moment’ and why it worked.

教学应用（SEL × 学术）Teaching Application (SEL × Academics)

  • 为课堂设计“安全提问机制”：匿名提问、同伴先答、老师最后总结。Build safe question channels: anonymous box, peer-first, teacher wrap-up.
  • 纠错三步：肯定努力→指出具体点→给一个可执行下一步。Correction in 3 steps: affirm effort → point to specifics → give an executable next step.
  • 英文句型：“Let’s work on this together—here’s one small next step.”English frame: “Let’s work on this together—here’s one small next step.”
