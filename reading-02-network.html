Lesson 2：你的大脑是一个网络 Lesson 2: Your Brain Is a Network

大脑功能来自动态网络协作，而不是固定‘某块区域=某个功能’。 Brain function comes from dynamic networks, not fixed ‘one region = one function.’

概念卡Concept cards 讨论Discussion 练习Practice

Big Idea 核心概念Big Idea

理解大脑要看“连接如何协作”。学习与情绪调节是多个网络共同完成的结果。 To understand the brain, look at coordination of connections. Learning and regulation emerge from multiple interacting networks.

Concept Cards 概念卡Concept Cards

  • 网络（Network）：多个区域形成动态协作系统。Network (网络): Multiple regions form dynamic cooperative systems.
  • 协同（Coordination）：不同网络按任务重新组合。Coordination (协同): Networks reconfigure depending on task.
  • 差异性（Variability）：不同人/不同天的网络状态不同。Variability (差异性): Network states vary across people and days.
  • 可塑性（Plasticity）：连接会因练习与经验改变。Plasticity (可塑性): Connections change with practice and experience.

理解展开Explanation

这给教育一个重要提醒：不要把学生固定成类型（‘你就是不擅长…’），网络是可训练、可恢复、可重建的。 Educational implication: don’t fix students into types (‘you’re just not good at…’). Networks are trainable, recoverable, rebuildable.

讨论问题Discussion

  • ‘网络’视角如何解释：同一个孩子在不同老师课上表现差异？How does a ‘network’ view explain a student performing differently with different teachers?
  • 你认为‘练习’在改变什么？知识？还是连接方式？What does practice change—facts, or connection patterns?
  • 如果网络会变，那我们如何设计反馈更有效？If networks change, what makes feedback more effective?

生活练习Practice

  • 本周选择一个技能（比如写作开头/画FBD），每天练 10 分钟，记录：更顺的点在哪里？Pick one skill this week (e.g., writing openings/FBD). Practice 10 min daily and note what becomes smoother.
  • 做一次‘换通道’练习：看→说→写→教别人，观察理解变化。Switch channels: see → say → write → teach—notice how understanding changes.
  • 给自己一个‘恢复网络’动作：散步 10 分钟再回来做题。Try a ‘network reset’: 10-minute walk, then return to the task.

教学应用（SEL × 学术）Teaching Application (SEL × Academics)

  • 给学生多通道输入输出：图像、口头、书面、动作。Use multi-modal I/O: visual, oral, written, embodied.
  • 把错误当作‘网络在尝试’：用提示让学生自己重组路径。Treat errors as ‘network attempts’: prompt students to re-route.
  • 教学生一句英文：“Let me re-route this—diagram first, then equations.”Teach an English line: “Let me re-route this—diagram first, then equations.”
